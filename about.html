About Me

I'm Emilia Michalek, a Computer Science undergraduate at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, with a strong interest in AI, backend development, and cybersecurity. I'm currently preparing to pursue a graduate degree focusing on cybersecurity.

I'm driven by curiosity - particularly in understanding how complex systems work and how technology can be leveraged to optimize workflows, improve user experiences, and solve challenging problems. I thrive in an environment that's constantly evolving, because it means I'm constantly learning.

Technical Background

I've worked across research and industry, from developing satellite-signal spoofing detection methods in an academic lab to building production-ready web application features at Motorola Solutions. In every role, I focus on making systems more efficient and intuitive - whether that means optimizing backend performance, refining UI/UX workflows, or bridging communication between globally distributed teams. My main tools are C++, C, Python, and Java, but I'm always expanding my toolkit.

I'm at my best when I'm building something that blends technical depth with user impact. I thrive in collaborative environments. My favorite moments are when I can take a rough idea, work with others to shape it, and turn it into a polished, usable product.

My Path to Computer Science

I've always been curious about how things are built - but software was the biggest mystery, so I dove straight into it. Even though it once felt intimidating, I saw that as the exact reason to study it: because I've found the most challenging problems often bring the greatest growth.

Alongside pursuing a path in CS, I also got the opportunity to participate in AFROTC which taught me invaluable skills including:

- Discipline and focused thinking

- Clear, concise communication

- Integrity, service, and pursuit of excellence

- The immense importance of soft skills
